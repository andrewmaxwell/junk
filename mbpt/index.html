Introverted (I): deriving energy from solitary or quiet environments, engaging in deep, thoughtful reflection, and often preferring meaningful one-on-one interactions over large social gatherings.
vs
Extraverted (E): drawing energy from interacting with others and the external environment, often enjoying socializing in larger groups, and tending to process thoughts and ideas through external discussion and activities.

Observing (S): preferring to focus on concrete, tangible information gathered through the senses and valuing practical applications and present realities over abstract or theoretical concepts.
vs
Intuitive: (N): preferring to focus on future possibilities and abstract theories, relying more on intuition and imagination than on concrete sensory data, and often enjoying exploring new ideas and innovative solutions.

Thinking (T): prioritizing logic and objective analysis in decision-making processes, valuing principles and truth over personal or emotional considerations, and tending to approach situations with a focus on consistency and rationality.
vs
Feeling (F): making decisions based on personal values and considerations of harmony and empathy, prioritizing human connections and emotions, and often assessing situations and actions based on their impact on others and oneself.

Prospecting (P): preferring a flexible, adaptable approach to life, staying open to new information and experiences, and often enjoying spontaneity and keeping options open rather than committing to a set plan or decision.
vs
Judging (J): preferring structure, organization, and decisiveness, valuing planned and orderly approaches to life, and often feeling more comfortable when decisions are made and a sense of closure is achieved.
